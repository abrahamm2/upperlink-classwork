Nigerian Jollof Rice

A flavourful one-pot West African classic

The Dish

Nigerian Jollof Rice — rich, smoky, and full of flavour

Ingredients

  • 2 cups long-grain parboiled rice
  • 400g tomato paste (or blended fresh tomatoes)
  • 2 red bell peppers, blended
  • 2 scotch bonnet peppers (adjust for spice level)
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 1 teaspoon curry powder
  • 1 teaspoon thyme
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 stock cube (e.g. Knorr)
Tip: Parboiled rice holds its shape better and gives Jollof Rice its signature firm texture. Avoid using soft or jasmine rice.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Blend the tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet, and half the onion together into a smooth paste.
  2. Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Fry the remaining chopped onion until soft and golden.
  3. Pour in the blended tomato mixture. Stir well and cook on medium heat for about 15–20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the raw smell is gone and the paste darkens slightly.
  4. Add the tomato paste and stir it in. Cook for another 5 minutes.
  5. Season with curry powder, thyme, bay leaves, stock cube, and salt. Stir everything together.
  6. Pour in the chicken stock and bring to a gentle boil.
  7. Wash the rice thoroughly and add it to the pot. Stir once to combine.
  8. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot tightly, and let the rice cook for 25–30 minutes.
  9. Check halfway through and add a splash of water if the rice looks too dry.
  10. Once the rice is cooked and fluffy, remove the bay leaves and serve hot. Enjoy!
